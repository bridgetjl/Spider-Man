Friends

Friends:

 Another reason Peter protects his identity is to protect his friends. He has made many long time friends throughout his life in high school, college, and The Daily Bugle. Here are just of few of his friends from his normal life.

Harry Osborn:

Harry Osborn, the son of wealthy business owner Norman Osborn, is in a longtime complicated relationship with Peter. The spider that bit Peter was from Oscorp’s, Norman Osborn’s business’, lab. Norman Osborn also goes on to become the legendary villain, the Green Goblin who has mission to kill Spider-Man. Harry, although not having the best relationship with his dad, goes on to fill the role of Green Goblin after Norman seemingly dies. Even through all this, Harry and Peter attempt to work out their friendship despite the struggles.

Mary Jane Watson:

Mary Jane Watson or MJ is an extremely important character in the Spider-Man universe. She and Peter have a very serious on and off relationship throughout their lives and her life is often in danger because of Peter’s role as Spider-Man. Peter first disregards her as a flaky party girl, but he gets to know her much better when they bond following the death of Gwen Stacy. In the Marvel Cinematic Universe MJ is changed to Michelle Jones, Peter's love interest who fills the role of MJ, but has very different qualities. She is very different from the orginal comic book character.

Flash Thompson:

Although Flash began as a bully to Peter, they later form a friendship and keep in touch throughout their lives.

Gwen Stacy:

Gwen Stacy was the first love of Peter Parker and was intended to be his main love interest until Mary Jane entered the scene. Gwen was tragically killed by the Green Goblin.

Ned Leeds and Betty Brant:
Ned and Betty are colleagues of Peter’s at the Daily Bugle. In the MCU, Ned Leeds and Betty Brant are two of Peter's friends from school and have a brief romance.
