Spider-Man

Bio:

 Spider-Man is a fictional superhero named Peter Parker. He is from Forest Hills, Queens and lives with his Aunt May. He was bitten by a radioactive spider and granted powers. He uses his powers for good by fighting crime and defeating villains. Created as a part of the Marvel Universe.

Origin Story:

When Peter was bitten he didn’t take his responsibility seriously at first. After the death of his Uncle Ben, at the hands of a robber Spider-Man had the opportunity to stop, he accepted his role and treated it carefully. His Uncle Ben was a huge inspiration to him and his very famous quote “With great power comes great responsibility”, fuels Peter’s motivation to fight crime.

Crime-fighting:

 Peter first began his role as a “friendly neighborhood Spider-Man” but went on to fight extremely powerful villains. Some of his most notable enemies include, The Green Goblin, Doctor Octopus, Venom, Mysterio, Vulture, Scorpion, and many more.

Movies:

 Peter Parker has been depicted in film by many different actors, most recently, Tom Holland. He is now portrayed in the Marvel Cinematic Universe along with other superheroes like Captain America and Iron Man and takes on enormous challenges like helping the Avengers to defeat Thanos. Through this journey, he himself has become an Avenger, after being taken under the wing of Tony Stark.

Normal Life:

 Although he is extremely busy with superhero activities, Peter makes time for his normal life. He, unlike many other heroes, chooses to keep his identity a secret in order to protect his family from his many foes. This means that he has a relatively normal life on the side. He attended Midtown High School and is extremely gifted in math and science, even creating his own web fluid and suit at first. After high school he attended the fictional Empire State University and then went on to a job at The Daily Bugle, a fictional newspaper, as a photographer; although he continued to take jobs in math and science as well. His role as Spider-Man allowed him the opportunity to take pictures directly at the scene of each crime he defeated. One downside to this job, however, is that he worked for editor J. Jonah Jameson, a vocal Spider-Man critic who often attempts to turn the public against Spider-Man. Something Spider-Man’s enemies have been trying to do for years.

Family:

 An important priority of Peter’s is protecting his family because he doesn’t have much left. Unfortunately, his parents died when he was very little and his Uncle Ben was killed by a robber.

Aunt May:

Peter’s Aunt May is a central character throughout the serious and is often in danger due to Peter’s superhero pursuits. He hides his identity from her but she eventually finds out and supports his good work. Spider-Man’s enemies in the past have used her to get to him by threatening her life and even at one point forcing Peter to erase his marriage to Mary Jane in order to protect his Aunt.

Uncle Ben:

Peter’s uncle is a huge inspiration to him and his death is the reason he began to use his powers for good.

Richard and Mary Parker:

Peter Parker’s parents who died when he was very young and left him in the care of his aunt and uncle.

Friends:

 Another reason Peter protects his identity is to protect his friends. He has made many long time friends throughout his life in high school, college, and The Daily Bugle. Here are just of few of his friends from his normal life.

Harry Osborn:

Harry Osborn, the son of wealthy business owner Norman Osborn, is in a longtime complicated relationship with Peter. The spider that bit Peter was from Oscorp’s, Norman Osborn’s business’, lab. Norman Osborn also goes on to become the legendary villain, the Green Goblin who has mission to kill Spider-Man. Harry, although not having the best relationship with his dad, goes on to fill the role of Green Goblin after Norman seemingly dies. Even through all this, Harry and Peter attempt to work out their friendship despite the struggles.

Mary Jane Watson:

Mary Jane Watson or MJ is an extremely important character in the Spider-Man universe. She and Peter have a very serious on and off relationship throughout their lives and her life is often in danger because of Peter’s role as Spider-Man. Peter first disregards her as a flaky party girl, but he gets to know her much better when they bond following the death of Gwen Stacy. In the Marvel Cinematic Universe MJ is changed to Michelle Jones, Peter's love interest who fills the role of MJ, but has very different qualities. She is very different from the orginal comic book character.

Flash Thompson:

Although Flash began as a bully to Peter, they later form a friendship and keep in touch throughout their lives.

Gwen Stacy:

Gwen Stacy was the first love of Peter Parker and was intended to be his main love interest until Mary Jane entered the scene. Gwen was tragically killed by the Green Goblin.

Ned Leeds and Betty Brant:

Ned and Betty are colleagues of Peter’s at the Daily Bugle. In the MCU, Ned Leeds and Betty Brant are two of Peter's friends from school and have a brief romance.
